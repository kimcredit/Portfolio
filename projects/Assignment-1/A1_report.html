Summary

Even though I didn't get as deep into the data and comparison as I would have liked, I did glean some insights. Fentanyl plays a larger role than it would appear at first glance, because even when it is not the leading cause of death, it is often a co-morbidity. Even though different demographic groups are dying from substance use at different amounts, the overall shape of distribution remains the same. This likely means that substance abuse has a cause tied to larger cultural events and trends rather than demographic views. Also, while it's good to see that young people are dying from substances less than older ones, some in that age range would have been too young to participate during the time frame observed. the 2020-2022 part of the young adults graph is approaching the same values as the early part of the next age range, suggesting that their low values right now may be higher as they get older and shift into the new demographic. Overall, I didn't get anywhere as far as I wanted with the analysis or exploration.
